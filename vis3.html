How does changing Latitude affect the Cloud cover?

The act of recording the percentage of cloud cover is an exciting study in the interaction of human nature and data quantification. Notice how the scatter of data seems to form neat lines across the graph. While there is no apparent correlation between latitude and cloud cover, these lines represent a correlation between the humans recording the data and the numbers we use in everyday life. We like numbers that are easy to use in math, which typically turn easily into fractions. Take a look at the data page! How many of the recorded Cloudiness percentages seem to be perfectly divisible by 25? How many by 10? What about 5? This quirky merger of human estimation with the hard science of data correlation teaches us to be cautious with the trends we observe.
