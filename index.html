But Why Latitude?

We may look at a Mercator map and go for a walk outside and it will never cross our mind that the earth isn't flat. Of course, we're AWARE that the Earth is round, but how often do we think about the ramifications of our celestial vessel being spherical?

Nearby (in the context of the universe), the Sun is emitting unthinkable amounts of radiation in all directions. As this radiation, light, and other particles strike the Earth, the angle at which it strikes the surface makes a tremendous influence on how much of the energy is absorbed and how much of it ricochets back into space.

Our planet has a thick atmosphere that wraps snugly around it. The nitrogen, oxygen, and Co2 mixture acts as both a shield and a lens absorbing particles from space and bending the light that passes through. The farther away from the equator, the steeper the angle that the light and particles hit the earth and the more atmosphere they need to pass through to do so.
