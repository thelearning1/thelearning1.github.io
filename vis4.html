How does changing Latitude affect the Wind speed?

Aside from an ever-so-slight trend perhaps whispering that wind might be a little faster the further you get from the equator, there is no useful correlation between latitude and the speed of the wind. Since wind speed is measured down to 1/100th of an mph, the data points are more cleanly spread here. This is an ideal place to notice how many more weather stations are north of the equator vs. south of it. Weather stations are usually near settlements. Most of the world's population is north of the equator as that's where most of the earth's landmass is. This provides ALL of the data with an interesting skew to the positive latitudes.
